Product & Feature Roadmap Q2 & Q3 2026

Author cestreitman@ Updated 05/30/2026 Status Draft
About this project

Flight Rising is a long-running browser-based pet/creature collection game where players breed, customize, and trade dragons, battle in a turn-based Coliseum, and take part in a recurring calendar of seasonal festivals. It has an active player-driven economy and a vocal community forum.

This is an independent portfolio exercise: a roadmap built outside the company, with no access to Flight Rising's data, codebase, or release pipeline. The three linked documents below are the resulting PRD and feature briefs.

01Context

This roadmap proposes prioritization for a set of player-requested improvements to Flight Rising, sourced from an independently-conducted ranked-choice voting survey of 97 active players. The survey ranked players' most-desired site feature and product additions across 50+ candidate features using three different RCV weighting systems; results were robust across all three. Full methodology and ranked results are documented here. This roadmap selects the top shipping candidates and explains sequencing across FR's 2026 release cycle for Q2 and Q3.

Note: this is an independent portfolio exercise; the author has no insider knowledge of FR's actual development roadmap, codebase, or release pipeline.

02Goals

  • Address the highest-ranked player-requested improvements from the survey
  • Stay within FR's existing Announcement Post release model; no new infrastructure dependencies
  • Ship at least one feature alongside an existing scheduled release (anniversary, major festival, or breed/apparel standard product release) to leverage natural narrative beats and minimize standalone announcement overhead

03Inputs & Evidence

Primary input: a ranked-choice survey of 97 self-selected active players, conducted via the Flight Rising Discussion subforum and a Google Forms instrument. Respondents ranked their top 5 desired improvements from a list of 50+ candidate features (curated from forum suggestions threads). Results were scored using three weighting systems—a GPA-style 5-4-3-2-1 scale, a traditional 10-9-8-7-6 scale, and an equal-weight 1-1-1-1-1 scale. The top 4 features were consistent across all three systems, supporting confidence in the ranking.

Secondary input: sustained sentiment in FR's Suggestions subforum and FRD subforum, where the top three features appear as recurring topics.

Acknowledged limitations: forum-only sampling under-represents privacy-conscious players, extremely casual non-forum users, and lapsed players. Self-selection bias likely amplifies signal from heavy/engaged users. The roadmap weights this input heavily because no comparable signal source exists for FR, but the sample bias should inform feature framing during eng review.

04Prioritization Framework

This roadmap uses RICE scoring, adapted to the FR context. Each candidate is scored on:

  • Reach: estimated share of monthly-active users (MAU) who would benefit, derived from survey ranking frequency and known player-segment overlap. Expressed as a rough percentage.
  • Impact: 3 (massive: addresses a daily pain point or has drastic consequences), 2 (high: addresses a frequent friction or adds moderate improvement), 1 (medium: modest improvement), 0.5 (low: niche improvement).
  • Confidence: 100% (direct survey evidence + forum corroboration), 80% (survey evidence alone), 50% (inferred).
  • Effort: rough person-weeks of eng + UX + PR time, estimated based on feature complexity.

RICE score = (Reach × Impact × Confidence) ÷ Effort. Higher scores ship sooner unless sequencing logic argues otherwise.

05Analysis & Sequencing Rationale

If we take all survey candidates with a RICE score above 50, we are left with "unretire year 1 fest items", "Inventory search in AH/PM/CR", "Press F to Fight on" keybind, and "Rebalance playground payouts".

We will, at the moment, go through the high-RICE entries and decide whether or not to pursue or shelve it.

Unretiring year 1 festival items: this is a simple policy change that FR can decide to execute. For this roadmap, we proceed with this feature, with the brief located here.
Inventory search in AH/PM/CR: a low-effort change that addresses a daily friction and should be easily implementable via legacy search functions already existing elsewhere in FR's codebase. We choose to implement, with the feature PRD located here.
Press F to Fight on: extremely simple QOL change that is highly requested. Expected low-effort, pending confirmation the transition surface is cleanly modifiable, and will please the playerbase. Highest RICE on the board (300) due to minimal effort. We ship it bundled with another release rather than standalone, as it doesn't warrant its own announcement. Feature brief here. No shipping urgency; it can ride along with another announcement, so its timing is flexible.
Rebalance playground payouts: the nature of how to rebalance should take little time by eng, but requires executive coordination and decision-making that would take several meetings. Appropriate rebalance rates, as well as whether or not to rebalance at all, is outside scope of the roadmap as it is fictional, and this feature's implementation requires specific insider knowledge. Shelved.

Other entries, we have decided to shelve for this roadmap, and why:

Dark theme / other themes: between the time of the survey and the time of the roadmap, this feature was implemented by the real-life FR team.
Official nest rental system: As per the RICE score, the effort is high. We can perhaps begin to work on it in Q3 2026, but it can't ship before Q4.
Ability to lock from breeding: Low-impact issue; modest improvement for a narrow collector use case (RICE 21, below threshold).
Color wheel expansion: significant effort from the art team, who is already working on other tasks at the moment, e.g. new breeds and apparel. Let them keep their current priorities.
Gene errors getting fixed asap: this would essentially require one artist to do constant fire drills as players inform staff of errors. Such a job is foreign to most artists, and difficult to hire. We would rather keep our current practices.

07Risks & Open Questions

  • Sample bias: The survey reached only forum-active players; a meaningful share of MAU never visits forums. Priorities for that segment may differ. If a real FR PM commissioned this work, they would supplement with in-game polling or behavioral data before committing to the sequence.
  • Effort estimates are external: All effort scores are inferred from feature complexity without codebase access. Real eng review may significantly adjust Effort (especially for inventory search, where the cost depends on whether legacy Customize-tab search code is still in the codebase — flagged in the PRD).
  • Veteran backlash on Y1 items: Unretiring scarce items will displease players who paid premium prices for them. The festival-items brief includes mitigation (slow drip via JSIC, sentiment monitoring), but this is the highest-risk feature for community reception.
  • Bundling capacity: All three features assume FR's Announcement Post model can accommodate them without delaying other planned releases. If announcement bandwidth is tighter than estimated, expect to drop the keybind to a later cycle (it's the smallest and easiest to defer).
